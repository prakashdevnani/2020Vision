CHALLENGES FACED

  1. There was a requirement to cut across party lines and be approved by Parliament to ensure continuity in its realization irrespective of the government of the day.
  2. Vision 2020, too, does not belong to any single party, government or individual.
  3. Once the government commits to realizing it, it has to be discussed and debated in detail by all elected representatives in Parliament so that a national consensus incorporating the concerns of all stakeholders such as the executive, the judiciary, the political class, media, intellectuals, academia, business, industry, teachers, doctors, farmers, and the youth of the nation emerges.
  4. Hence the elected leader of the nation the driving force behind the vision should be a creative leader who walks an unexplored path of developmental politics with the cooperation of other parties, using the core competences of other leaders, intellectuals, able and creative minds from all disciplines irrespective of their party affiliations, to realize the vision.
